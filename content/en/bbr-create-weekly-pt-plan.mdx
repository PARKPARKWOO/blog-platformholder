If you've ever spent Sunday night thinking "what should I train this week?" — and then defaulted to the same routine, or skipped the week entirely — you're not alone. The hardest part of consistency isn't motivation, it's **decision fatigue**.

BBR (Barbell Robot) was built so the AI handles that decision for you. **Pick goals, days, and time — your weekly PT plan is ready every Monday.**

## Why automated planning matters

- The biggest reason consistent gym-goers fall off isn't willpower; it's the cost of choosing what to do.
- Removing that cost lowers the barrier to walking into the gym.
- AI applying progressive overload from your past sessions reduces plateaus.

## 5 steps to your first PT plan

### Step 1 — Install + Kakao sign-in

Search "BBR" on the App Store / Google Play. Sign in with Kakao once and your profile is synced.

### Step 2 — Pick goals

- Weight loss / strength / endurance — pick one or more.
- Adjustable later in My Page.

> Start with 1–2 to stay focused.

### Step 3 — Available days + session length

- Check the days you can train (Mon–Sun).
- Enter session duration (40 / 50 / 60 / 90 min).

This drives whether the AI suggests a 2-split, 3-split, or 5-split routine.

### Step 4 — Generate the weekly PT plan

- Tap "Generate PT plan". The Gemini-based engine returns a full week in ~30 seconds.
- Each day shows exercises, sets, and reps.
- Don't like an exercise? Swap it from the library.

### Step 5 — First session at the gym + logging

- Open today's plan; weights from past sessions pre-fill.
- Hit "Finish" — duration and calories are auto-tracked.

Next Monday's plan applies **progressive overload** based on your records.

## Wrap-up

The reason most weeks slip isn't time — it's the weight of deciding. **This week's PT? Already done.** That's the one job BBR takes off your plate.
